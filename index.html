about me

I am a third year Ph.D. student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at University of Utah, Salt Lake City, UT, USA. I joined "The U" during Fall 2016, where I am currently advised by Prof. Berardi Sensale-Rodriguez and co-adviced by Prof. Rajesh Menon. Before moving to Utah, I completed my bachelor's degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from West Bengal University of Technology, Kolkata, WB, India where I was advised by Prof. Arpan Deyasi.

My current research interests are in the areas of diffractive optics, metamaterials, plasmonics, nanotechnology and nanophotonics, with particular focus on computational approaches aimed towards the design of engineered nano-structures. My work spans a broad range of topics including design of planar passive visible/THz optical elements right up to planar reconfigurable active nano-scale optical devices designed using digital metamaterials and advanced metasurfaces. In this context, I have also extensively worked on translating and exploiting well-established methods and concepts from computer science engineering to the realm of optics and photonics. Nonetheless, my work includes looking into several different optimization algorithms as well as machine learning tools and techniques for possible use in designing nano-scale photonic and optical devices.

My work in general, intends to cater to the growing computational need for most computationally complicated designed visible/THz devices to avoid having to build up much computational pressure which is expensive, time consuming, and not scalable.
